The Life of the Bard

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world's pre-eminent dramatist. Born in Stratford-upon-Avon, his surviving works consist of some 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and three long narrative poems.

His plays have been translated into every major living language and are performed more oft than those of any other playwright. His early plays were mainly comedies and histories, fields he raised to the peak of sophistication and artistry by the end of the 16th century.

Major Milestones

1564

Early Life & Birth

Born in Stratford-upon-Avon to John Shakespeare, a leather merchant, and Mary Arden.

1592

Establishment in London

By this year, Shakespeare was earning a living as an actor and a playwright in London, catching the attention of critics.

1599

The Globe Theatre

Shakespeare's playing company built the Globe Theatre on the south bank of the River Thames, hosting his greatest masterpieces.

1616

Final Act

Died at the age of 52 in his hometown of Stratford-upon-Avon, leaving behind a monumental literary legacy.

Enduring Masterpieces

The Tragedies

Deep psychological character studies including Hamlet, Macbeth, Othello, and King Lear.

The Comedies

Witty wordplay and complex romances like A Midsummer Night's Dream and Much Ado About Nothing.

The Sonnets

A collection of 154 poems dealing with themes such as the passage of time, love, beauty, and mortality.
